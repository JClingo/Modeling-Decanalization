\title{Modeling Decanalization with Homeostatic Reinforcement Learning}
\author{Joshua Clingo and Jeff Yoshimi}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We present a computational proof-of-principle model of canalization, decanalization, and recanalization in a homeostatic navigation task. Agents learned to manage a single homeostatic variable in a tiled environment where repeated traversal reduced movement costs and promoted entrenched behavioral paths. We compared homeostatic reinforcement learning (HRL) with a negative feedback (NF) reward function under matched canalization and decanalization conditions. Decanalization was modeled as a temporary composite intervention that altered perceived reward, perceived traversal cost, and the rate and depth of canalization. In this setting, HRL agents showed improved post-decanalization homeostatic performance relative to matched no-decanalization controls, whereas NF agents did not. We offer the model as a formal instantiation of one candidate mechanism by which temporary destabilization can shift exploration and subsequent behavior in a constrained environment. The results are intended to clarify computational assumptions behind canalization-style frameworks, not to provide a direct model of psychiatric disorders or psychedelic therapy.
\end{abstract}
